GHANIAN NAMING CULTURE

Akan is the language of the people called Akans. The Akans are the largest ethnic group in Ghana. The names are dependant on the day that the male or female was born.The naming system of the Akan people is as distinct as that which elders in Togo (the Ewe people), the Ga people, Benin (the Fon people), and the African diaspora pass on to toddlers. Other forms of traditional naming include middle names, which can refer to order of birth as part of the caste of siblings, twin status, or could be an inherited maiden name.
